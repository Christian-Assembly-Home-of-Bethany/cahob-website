Our Belief

Our Faith and Vision

The foundation of our life together

We believe in the one true Sovereign God, who is omniscient, omnipotent, and omnipresent (Ps. 139:7–10; Ps. 147:5; Rev. 19:6); and in the Divine Trinity (Matt. 28:19; 2 Cor. 13:14).

We believe that the Triune God created mankind in His image and likeness to express and represent Him (Gen. 1:26–28), not only individually but corporately as the Church (Eph. 2:19–22; 1 Pet. 2:5). The Church is the Body of Christ, with Christ as its Head (Eph. 1:22–23; Col. 1:18). Through the work of the Holy Spirit, Christ is sanctifying and cleansing His Church so that she may be presented to Him as a glorious Church, His Bride (Eph. 5:25–27; Rev. 19:7–8).

God's eternal purpose for humanity has never changed (Eph. 1:9–12; Eph. 3:10–11). Although all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God (Rom. 3:23), in His love and mercy God gave us His Son, not only to make atonement for our sins (Rom. 5:8, 1 Pet. 2:24; 1 John 2:2), but also that we might receive His divine life (John 10:10; 1 John 5:11–12) and be conformed to the image of His Son (Rom. 8:29; 2 Pet. 1:4). Whoever believes in the Lord Jesus Christ is saved, born again, and receives the life of God within (John 3:16, John 3:3–6, John 1:12–13, Acts 16:31).

Through Christ's finished work, our sins are forgiven (Eph. 1:7; Col. 2:13–14), we are no longer under condemnation (Rom. 8:1), and our old man, which was enslaved to sin, has been crucified with Christ (Rom. 6:6). Furthermore, if we have been united with Him in the likeness of His death, we shall also be united with Him in the likeness of His resurrection (Rom. 6:5–6).

Because of this, man's own abilities, wisdom, strength, and efforts cannot satisfy God, no matter how well-intentioned, sincere, capable, zealous, efficient, or even outwardly successful (John 15:5; Rom. 7:18; Rom. 8:8; Gal. 2:20). Only the life of Christ expressed through us is pleasing to Him (Phil. 2:13; Col. 1:27). “But we have this treasure in earthen vessels, that the excellence of the power may be of God and not of us” (2 Cor. 4:7). Therefore, we seek to deny ourselves, take up our cross daily, and follow Christ (Luke 9:23; Matt. 16:24). For those who have been born again, this becomes our moment-by-moment choice to not live according to the flesh, but according to the Spirit (Rom. 8:4–9). By the Holy Spirit, we live by the divine life of Christ within us, for His life is altogether different from our natural life (Phil. 1:21; Gal. 2:20; Col. 3:4; John 6:63). As we walk by the Spirit, Christ is formed in us and manifested through us (Gal. 5:16, 25; Gal. 4:19).

For this reason, Christ Jesus as Lord is our only message (2 Cor. 4:5). Our hope of glory is that Christ would rule and reign in our hearts and be fully expressed through our lives, individually and corporately (Col. 1:27; Eph. 3:16–19; Gal. 2:20). We desire to be known only as those who belong to Christ (1 Cor. 6:19–20; Phil. 3:8–10), seeking to cooperate with the work of the Holy Spirit (Phil. 2:13; Gal. 5:16, 25), so that God's eternal purpose may be made known through His Church in the present age (Eph. 3:8–11; 1 Tim. 3:15).
