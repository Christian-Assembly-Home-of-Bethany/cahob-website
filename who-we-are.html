Who We Are

Learning to live “no longer I, but Christ”

We are a group of believers who, through prayer, fellowship, and daily life, are learning to live according to the reality of “no longer I, but Christ” (Gal. 2:20). Following our Lord, who said, “The Son can do nothing of Himself; He can do only what he sees his Father doing” (John 5:19), we also seek to stand in the position of self-denial and nothingness before God, receiving Christ as our source, our life, and our supply (John 15:5; Col. 3:11).

We are all sinners saved by grace (Eph. 2:8–9). Therefore, whenever we gather, we come not trusting in ourselves or by our own abilities but in the position of nothingness before God, just as our Lord stood in the River of Jordan (Matt. 3:13–17). Our desire is that the heavens would be opened, the Holy Spirit would have the free way among us, and that, by holding fast to Christ, all who come might touch the Lord and come to know God.

We give our Lord Jesus Christ the preeminence in all things (Col. 1:18). He is all, and in all (Col. 3:11). He is our Lord and our Head. We receive every circumstance from His sovereign hand as an opportunity to learn Christ. In each situation, we seek to cease from ourselves, turn to Him, receive His present salvation, and remain in fellowship and communion with Him. Our desire is to live in continual, fresh dependence upon Christ: not only when we gather together, but also in our homes, our families, our workplaces, our schools, and throughout our daily lives.

We are not affiliated with any denomination, but gladly welcome fellowship with all who seek to know Christ and God’s eternal purpose. Together, we desire to encourage one another (1 Thess. 5:11), to be built up together as His Body (Eph. 4:15–16), and to bear a true testimony of Jesus Christ in this generation (Phil. 2:15–16).
